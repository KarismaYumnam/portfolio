Karisma Yumnam

Karisma Yumnam is a 3rd year PhD student in the Department of Biosystems Engineering at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. Her research focuses on developing data-driven models for in-season crop yield forecasting at plot scale using remotely sensed phenotyping data. Her long term research interests is to develop explainable artifical intelligence systems that leverage open-source earth observation data to support agricultural decisions. Aside from her research, she also works with undergraduate students, high school teachers and students to share data science and agriculture concepts through Cultivate ACCESS to Data Science program at UNL.

In personal life, she is a multi-faceted individual with diverse pursuits. She is a constantly imporving tennis player (NTRP rating: 3.0-3.5), watercolor artist and a 700+ Chess player. She enjoys biking, hiking, reading, gardening and ocasionally cooking new recipes!
